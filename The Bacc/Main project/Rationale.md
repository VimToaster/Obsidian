However, I am aware that most people are not entirely familiar with the concept of quantum computers, so I would like to spend a few lines explaining the basics. Firstly, the word 'quantum' is used to describe the basic nature of the machines. This word comes from an area of physics that describes small *things* such as electrons or photons. This is because at at these scales, the concepts of traditional or Newtonian physics begin to break down, therefore resulting in the advent of a new field of physics to describe them called quantum physics. The basic concept of this field is that, at a certain point when particles pass the atomic barrier, aka subatomic. These particles then behave entirely differently to those of particles of scales larger than this size. For instance, when using an electron beam to shoot the particles through two slits, they will act as a wave. We can prove this by using a material that detects an electron passing through it, and when doing to in this setup, it will produce several areas of high and low frequency (Fig.1). However, when recreating a the exact same setup, rather this time placing a device that detects whether an electron passes through either one of the slits. (Pretend there's a beep whenever it detects an electron). This same setup instead produces two lines. (Fig.2)

This is the basic principle of quantum mechanics and hence quantum computers. The theory that a given particle can act as both a wave and a particle. Though this specific behaviour is not exactly used within quantum computers, ideas that stem from it are. For instance, when passing through the two slits in the first experiment, physicists wanted and explanation for why the pattern occurs, resulting in Heisenberg's famous wavefunction to describe the *superposition* of a particle (we can't call it a position as the result of his equation is a probability rather than a discrete number). All of this is rather complex, however it is this idea that is crucially important for the invention of quantum computing. If we can somehow maintain the particle in this state if superposition, we can calculate solutions to problems that require pinpointing one solution out of many. For example passwords. If we can maintain the particle's state, they can exist in a superposition where among all possible states, one of which will result in the correct answer.
