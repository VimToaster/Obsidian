
# Key ideas
- Rejects the positivistic notion of deviants simply reacting to external forces
	- Looks at interaction between deviants and those who define them as deviant
		- i.e. interaction between deviants, parents, teachers, doctors, police, judges and probation officers
	- Recognises meanings are not fixed and clear-cut


# Goffman
- Social labels effect out perception of people, e.g. a 'stigma'
- Defines a stigma as an "attribute that is deeply discrediting"
	- General thesis is that stigma is a form of deviance tat leads to judge individuals as illegitimate for participation in an interaction
- Identified three main types of stigma:
	- 1. Stigma associated with mental illness
	- 2. Stigma associated with physical deformation
	- 3. stigma attached to identification with a particular race, ethnicity, religion, ideology etc
