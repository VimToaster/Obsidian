# GROVER
- Abbreviation for:
	- Generalisation
	- Reliability
	- Objectivity
	- Validity
	- Ethics
	- Representativeness

# Interpretivists
- People who generally prefer qualitative methods over quantitative
	- Prioritise validity over generalisation/representativeness
	- People are more complex than just statistics, numbers cannot fully describe the complexity of human behaviour
	- Rapport/Verstehen

# Positivists
- People who generally prefer quantitative methods
	- Prioritise generalisation/representativeness over validity
	- Social facts
	- View sociology as more of a scientific study where numbers can accurately describe people

# Rapport
- In accord, harmony, or sympathy; having a mutual, especially a private, understanding; in mesmerism, in that relation of sympathy which permits influence or communication.
	- Positivists like
	- Gained through qual methods

# BSA
- British sociological association guidelines from 2017
	- Must adhere to the rules provided for research to be published
- INFORMED CONSENT - Important
- If >18 must contact parents
- Right to withdraw
- Anonymity


# Secondary data
- Data taken from external studies
	- Used for referencing and literature review in 30 marker

# Quantitative research
- Research that gathers data in the form of statistics

# Qualitative research
- Research that gathers data in the form of spoken words

# Ethics
- Whether a study respects the subjects rights and aligns with the [[#BSA]]
- Part of [[#GROVER]]

# Generalisability
- How well a study can be applied to a greater population
- Applies to [[#Quantitative research]]

# Reliability
- How well a study can be repeated
- Applies to [[#Quantitative research]]
# Operationalisation
- Defining key terms when producing a study
	- e.g. adults referring to people >18

# Objectivity
- How free from bias a study is
- [[#Quantitative research]] tends to be more objective

# Validity
- How honest the responses are
- [[#Qualitative research]]'s main benefit over [[#Quantitative research]]

# Official statistics
- Statistics published by official sources such as the [[#ONS]]

# ONS
- Office of national statistics
- Reliable source of [[#Official statistics]]

# Questionnaire surveys
- A source of [[#Quantitative research]]
- Often limited responses
- - Lack validity
- + Objective

# Unstructured interview
- Source of [[#Quantitative research]]
- Researcher interviews a person based on a general topic rather than direct questions
- + Validity
- - Objectivity
	- Subject could be influenced by actions of the researcher

# Structured interview
- Person to person source of [[#Qualitative research]]
- Researcher asks subject structured questions
	- Cannot probe answers
- - Validity
