
# Aim
- Outline the aim of the study - link to context and the method you have selected
	- Should be a few sentences

# Define an operationalise
- Highlight key terms of the group/individuals in relation to the context of the scenario#
	- Usually 2 terms to operationalise
		- Couple of sentences
			- Topic
			- Sample
- Literature review
	- Review existing literature relating the the subject and how your study will expand on the information provided

# Theory
- 2/3 page paragraph
	- Key terms used throughout
		- [[Dictionary#GROVER]] terms
		- Justify why you are using the methods, be postive
		- Link methods you have used
			- Favoured by [Interpretivists](Dictionary#Interpretivists)/[Positivists](Dictionary#Positivists)
				- Describe why
				- Link theory in detail

# Ethics
- 2/3 page paragraph
- Consider deception, informed consent, anonymity, confidentiality, sensitivity, avoiding harm
	- Use of pseudonyms in qualitative data
	- + [Rapport](Dictionary#Rapport)
	- [BSA](Dictionary#BSA)

# Sample/method sampling

- Short paragraph
- Access to sample?
- Gatekeeper?
	- Person who gatekeeps the individuals you are studying
- Strengths and weaknesses?
- What sampling method will you deploy?
- Why have you used this?
- How will you access the sample?
- How will you contact them?
- Will you need to contact parents?
- Research is small scale
	- Teenagers?
	- Adults?
	- Geriatrics?
	- Mothers?

# Pilot study
- Small paragraph
- Test interview questions
	- No ambiguity
- Interview
	- Ice breaking questions
	- Test questions with teachers/friends
